# About me
I generally suck at programming, but I'm a great software engineer. I've worked with all sorts of tools and frameworks in various support 
of all parts of the SDLC, and there's even more I haven't worked with. Let's change that!

My start with computers was late. I didn't have my own access to a computer until college, a crappy 2010 Windows laptop. The only thing I knew going into college was I liked math. A man at freshmen orientation spouted something about "avoiding software engineering if you have no background", so I chose a double-major in Mechanical and Aerospace engineering. I took one C++ class, learned some Matlab by necessity.

I had a mechanical engineering internship where the most useful thing I learned was the I didn't want to be a mechanical engineer anymore. I had discovered Blockchain technology and thus started my love for Computer Science. I started toying with Android programming in my free time on my potato machine, continued my ME/AE courses, and graduated with my B.S.es...

My first job out of college was technical writing. I moved across the country, started the next chapter, with this love of computers waxing and waning in the background. I continued the Android programming (in the end, exposing me to many full-stack concepts way too early).

Eventually, I decided to take my shot. I contacted a software manager at my company, stating my interest in his Autopilot group (my senior project was an Autopilot program...more Matlab). He told me to learn C and he'd interview me. One K&R book and three months later, I had that interview and soon after, my first job in the software industry.

Within 3 years, I had transistioned from Associate Developer to Senior Engineer on a newly formed DevOps group as one of the first hires. It was here I found my passion: working on software to help **`real programmers`** build.
